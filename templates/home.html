A Day On The Kayal

How a Kayal cruise unfolds

Every booking follows roughly the same rhythm — the only variable is how slowly you let it move.

01 / Noon

Boarding at the jetty

Step aboard at Alleppey or Kumarakom, settle into your cabin, and watch the crew cast off into Vembanad Lake.

02 / Afternoon

Paddy fields & coir villages

Cruise past coconut groves and below-sea-level paddy fields, with a stop to see coir rope being made by hand.

03 / Evening

Sunset anchor

The boat anchors in a quiet stretch of water for sunset, followed by a candle-lit Kerala thali on deck.

04 / Morning

Sunrise & return

Wake to tea on the sundeck, a light breakfast, and a gentle cruise back to the jetty by mid-morning.

Why Kayal

Built around the boat, not the booking

We operate a small, well-maintained fleet ourselves — so the boat in the photos is the boat that arrives.

Private crew, every trip

A dedicated captain, chef, and deckhand stay with your boat for the full booking — no shared crews, no rushed turnarounds.

Home-cooked Kerala meals

Meals are cooked fresh on board using local fish, vegetables, and spices — breakfast, lunch, evening tea, and dinner included.

AC cabins & open sundecks

Every cabin has air-conditioning and an attached bathroom, with a shared sundeck for the parts of the day you'll want outside.

Eco-certified boats

Our fleet runs on low-emission engines with on-board waste treatment, so the backwaters stay clean for the next crew of travelers.
